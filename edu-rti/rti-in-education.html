Application of RTI in Education Sector

The Right to Information Act, 2005 has emerged as a powerful tool for promoting transparency and accountability in India's education sector. Educational institutions, being significant recipients of public funds and serving the crucial public function of imparting education, fall squarely within the ambit of the RTI Act.

All educational institutions established by law, owned, controlled, or substantially financed by government funds are considered "public authorities" under Section 2(h) of the RTI Act and are obligated to provide information under the Act.

Educational Institutions Covered Under RTI

Government Institutions
  • Central Universities
  • IITs, NITs, IIMs
  • Government Schools
  • Kendriya Vidyalayas
  • State Universities
  • Government Colleges
Institutions Receiving Substantial Funding
  • Aided Private Schools
  • Deemed Universities
  • Institutions receiving UGC grants
  • Institutions with government-sponsored scholarships
  • Research institutions with public funding
Private Institutions: Purely private educational institutions that do not receive substantial government funding are generally not covered under the RTI Act. However, they may still be subject to RTI in specific contexts where they perform public functions.

Landmark RTI Cases in Education

CBSE vs. Aditya Bandopadhyay (2011)

Issue: Access to evaluated answer sheets

Decision: The Supreme Court ruled that evaluated answer sheets are "information" under the RTI Act and must be provided to students upon request.

Impact: This landmark judgment transformed examination transparency across all educational institutions in India, empowering students to access their evaluated answer scripts.

Read Full Case
Delhi University vs. Central Information Commission (2017)

Issue: Disclosure of BA degree records of a public figure

Decision: The Delhi High Court ruled that educational degrees of public officials are not exempt from disclosure under privacy provisions of the RTI Act.

Impact: Established that educational qualifications of public figures are matters of public interest, not protected by privacy exemptions.

Read Full Case
Prashant Singh vs. IIM Ahmedabad (2016)

Issue: Disclosure of CAT percentile calculation methodology

Decision: The CIC directed IIM to disclose the complete methodology of calculating CAT percentiles and selection process details.

Impact: Led to greater transparency in entrance examination processes for professional courses across institutions.

Read Full Case
Parents Association vs. DPS Society (2019)

Issue: Fee structure transparency in private aided schools

Decision: The CIC ruled that schools receiving land at concessional rates or other substantial benefits from the government are public authorities under RTI.

Impact: Expanded the scope of RTI to include partially aided private educational institutions receiving indirect benefits.
