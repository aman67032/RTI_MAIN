Evaluation Records & RTI

The Supreme Court of India, in its landmark judgment in CBSE vs. Aditya Bandopadhyay (2011), established that evaluated answer scripts are "information" under the RTI Act. This judgment revolutionized transparency in educational evaluation, empowering students to access their evaluated answer sheets and understand how they were assessed.

Supreme Court held: "The evaluated answer book of an examinee is a document containing the 'opinion' of the examiner. Normally, the opinion of an expert or opinion expressed in a subjective evaluation, cannot be subject to disclosure or scrutiny. But the evaluated answer book does not represent the opinion of the examiner simpliciter, but the opinion of the examiner as to whether the answers given by the candidate are correct or wrong, and the marks deserved by the answers... Therefore, an evaluated answer book is not completely exempt from disclosure under the RTI Act."

Educational Ministry Circular

Following the Supreme Court judgment, the Ministry of Education issued circular F.No.2-8/2019-TS.I (May 17, 2019) providing detailed guidelines for educational institutions on:

  • Providing access to evaluated answer sheets under RTI
  • Standardizing procedures for inspection of answer sheets
  • Maintaining digital copies of answer sheets when requested
  • Disclosing evaluation schemes and model answers after examinations
  • Establishing transparent re-evaluation procedures

Types of Evaluation Information Accessible

Examination Answer Sheets
  • Evaluated answer sheets with examiner's marks and comments
  • Marks awarded for each question/section
  • Moderation applied, if any
  • Examiner's annotations and corrections
  • Total marks calculation and verification
Evaluation Scheme
  • Model/sample answers for reference
  • Marking scheme with marks distribution
  • Evaluation guidelines provided to examiners
  • Grading criteria and cutoffs
  • Answer key for objective questions
Result Processing
  • Statistical distribution of marks
  • Moderation criteria and implementation
  • Scaling methodology (if applicable)
  • Grace marks policy and application
  • Result tabulation procedures
Administrative Records
  • Minutes of examination committee meetings
  • Examiner selection criteria and qualifications
  • Re-evaluation/review process details
  • Complaints handled and resolutions
  • Rules governing examinations and evaluations
Important: While information about evaluation processes is generally accessible, there may be limitations on accessing evaluated answer sheets of other students due to privacy concerns under Section 8(1)(j) of the RTI Act.

Process for Accessing Evaluation Records

1
Identify the Correct PIO

File your RTI application with the PIO of the examining body (board, university, institute) that conducted the examination.

2
Provide Specific Details

Include your roll number, examination name/code, date of exam, subject, and other identifying details to help locate your answer sheet.

3
Request Both Answer Sheet and Marking Scheme

For maximum benefit, request both your evaluated answer sheet and the marking scheme/model answers used for evaluation.

4
Pay the Required Fee

RTI application fee (₹10) plus additional charges for photocopies (typically ₹2 per page for answer sheets).

5
Choose Inspection Option

You can request physical inspection first (which is free) before asking for copies to reduce costs.

6
Follow Up for Timely Response

The PIO must respond within 30 days. If denied, file a first appeal with the appellate authority of the institution.

Sample RTI for Evaluation Records

APPLICATION UNDER RIGHT TO INFORMATION ACT, 2005

To,
The Public Information Officer
[Name of Educational Institution/Examination Board]
[Complete Address]

Subject: Request for information regarding evaluated answer sheet under RTI Act, 2005

1. Name of the Applicant: [Your Name]

2. Address: [Your Complete Postal Address]

3. Contact Details: [Phone Number and Email]

4. Details of Information Sought:

  1. Certified copy of my evaluated answer sheet for [Subject Name], [Examination Name], held on [Examination Date] with Roll Number [Your Roll Number].
  2. Copy of the marking scheme/model answers used for evaluation of the above-mentioned answer sheet.
  3. Information regarding the moderation policy applied, if any, for the above examination.
  4. Details of the qualifications of the examiner who evaluated my answer sheet.
  5. Copy of any rules/regulations governing the evaluation process for the aforementioned examination.

5. Period to which the information relates: [Academic Year/Examination Period]

6. Whether information is required by post or in person: By post/in person

7. I am enclosing an application fee of ₹10/- by [mode of payment].

8. I hereby state that the information sought does not fall within the restrictions contained in Section 8 and 9 of the RTI Act and to the best of my knowledge, it pertains to your office.

Date: [Current Date]

Place: [Your City]

Signature of the Applicant

[Your Name]

Common Challenges & Solutions

Some institutions claim that evaluation is an "internal process" exempt from RTI.

Solution: Cite the Supreme Court judgment in CBSE vs. Aditya Bandopadhyay which clearly establishes that evaluated answer sheets are information under the RTI Act and not exempt.

Educational bodies sometimes charge excessive fees for providing copies of answer sheets.

Solution: First request inspection of records (which is free), then select only relevant pages for photocopying. If fees are still excessive, file a complaint with the Information Commission.

Institutions may claim that answer sheets have been disposed of or are not available.

Solution: Request the record retention policy for answer sheets. Most institutions must maintain answer sheets for at least 3-6 months after results (some for longer periods). If destroyed before the policy period, this can be grounds for appeal.

Some institutions provide answer sheets but refuse to share marking schemes.

Solution: In your first appeal, cite CIC decision (CIC/CBSEX/A/2016/305296) which mandates that marking schemes are essential information for understanding evaluation and should be provided along with answer sheets.
