About DoPT Guidelines

The Department of Personnel and Training (DoPT) is the nodal department for implementation of the Right to Information Act, 2005 in India. As the administrative ministry for the RTI Act, DoPT issues guidelines, clarifications, and instructions to ensure consistent implementation of the Act across all public authorities.

DoPT guidelines are issued under Section 25(2) of the RTI Act and provide clarity on procedural aspects not explicitly covered in the Act.

Significance of DoPT Guidelines

DoPT guidelines serve as a reference point for all public authorities in the following ways:

  • They provide uniform interpretation of provisions in the RTI Act
  • They standardize procedural aspects of RTI implementation
  • They address common queries and challenges faced by Public Information Officers
  • They incorporate judicial pronouncements and CIC decisions into implementation framework
  • They update implementation practices based on emerging challenges and technological advancements

Key DoPT Guidelines

OM No.: 10/2/2009-IR

Date: June 12, 2009

View Full Guideline

Key Points:

  • RTI applications should be transferred to the appropriate public authority within 5 days under Section 6(3)
  • The entire application should be transferred, not just a part of it
  • The transfer should be communicated to the applicant immediately
  • If information is held by multiple authorities, the application should be transferred to all relevant authorities
  • The 30-day timeline for providing information starts from the date of receipt by the appropriate authority

OM No.: 10/9/2008-IR

Date: July 27, 2010

View Full Guideline

Key Points:

  • Detailed procedure for handling third-party information under Section 11
  • Third party should be given notice and opportunity to make representation within 10 days
  • PIO must consider third party's representation before making a decision
  • Final decision must be communicated to the third party
  • Information should not be disclosed until the appeal period is over or appeal is decided

OM No.: 1/6/2011-IR

Date: April 15, 2013

View Full Guideline

Key Points:

  • Comprehensive guidance on proactive disclosure under Section 4(1)(b)
  • Templates for disclosing various categories of information
  • Guidelines for digital publication of Section 4 information
  • Requirement for regular updating of proactively disclosed information
  • Suggestions for making disclosures more accessible and user-friendly

OM No.: 1/3/2016-IR

Date: March 31, 2017

View Full Guideline

Key Points:

  • Instructions for handling RTI applications received through the online RTI portal
  • Procedure for integration of public authorities with the RTI online portal
  • Guidelines for electronic transfer of applications between public authorities
  • Process for electronic payment of RTI fees
  • Timeline for responding to online RTI applications

Frequently Asked Questions

While DoPT guidelines are not laws in themselves, they are considered authoritative interpretations of the RTI Act. Public authorities typically follow these guidelines, and the CIC often refers to them in its decisions.

DoPT guidelines are issued as needed based on implementation challenges, court orders, or CIC decisions. On average, several guidelines are issued each year on various aspects of RTI implementation.

DoPT guidelines are primarily meant for central government public authorities. However, many state governments adopt similar guidelines or use them as a reference for their own implementation.
