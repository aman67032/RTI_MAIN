About RTI Fee Structure

The Right to Information (Regulation of Fee and Cost) Rules, 2005, as amended by the RTI Rules, 2012, prescribe the fees and costs associated with filing RTI applications and obtaining information. These guidelines ensure that the process of obtaining information remains affordable while covering reasonable costs of providing information.

The RTI Act exempts persons below poverty line (BPL) from paying any fees for information. BPL applicants must submit a copy of their BPL card along with their application.

Payment Methods

Cash payment can be made directly at the counter of the public authority. Always ask for a receipt of your payment. This method is suitable when applying in person.

Never send cash through post for RTI applications.

You can pay RTI fees through a Demand Draft (DD) or Banker's Cheque drawn in favor of the Accounts Officer or designated officer of the concerned public authority.

Important: Check the exact payee name from the public authority's website before making the DD/Banker's Cheque.

Indian Postal Order (IPO) is the most commonly used method for paying RTI application fees when applying by post. IPOs can be purchased from any post office.

  1. Purchase an IPO of ₹10 from any post office
  2. Fill in the name of "Accounts Officer" or designated officer of the concerned public authority as the payee
  3. Attach the IPO with your RTI application
IPOs are valid for six months from the date of issue.

Some state governments allow payment of RTI application fees through court fee stamps. This method is particularly common in states like Maharashtra, Tamil Nadu, and Uttar Pradesh.

This method is generally not applicable for Central Government public authorities. Check specific state RTI rules before using this method.

For RTI applications filed through the RTI Online Portal (https://rtionline.gov.in), payment can be made online through:

  • Internet Banking
  • Credit/Debit Cards
  • UPI
Online payment is the quickest method for paying RTI fees and is available 24/7. The receipt is generated instantly, and there is no need for physical paperwork.
Note: Different public authorities may have specific preferences for payment methods. Always check the public authority's website or contact them to confirm the accepted modes of payment.

BPL Fee Exemption

Citizens below the poverty line (BPL) are exempt from paying any fees for filing RTI applications and obtaining information.

Required Documents:
  • Copy of BPL card/certificate
  • Self-declaration stating BPL status
BPL exemption applies only to the person whose name is on the BPL card/certificate.
