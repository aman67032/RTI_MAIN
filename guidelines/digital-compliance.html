Digital Compliance Under RTI

As government systems increasingly move online, digital compliance has become a crucial aspect of RTI implementation. Digital compliance refers to the standards and practices that public authorities must follow to ensure their digital systems support RTI obligations effectively.

The Department of Personnel and Training (DoPT) and the Ministry of Electronics and Information Technology (MeitY) have issued various guidelines to ensure that digital systems are RTI-compliant and accessible to all citizens.

Key Aspects of Digital Compliance

Online Accessibility

Ensuring RTI portals and information are accessible to all citizens, including those with disabilities.

Data Security

Protecting sensitive information while ensuring transparency in digital systems.

Digital Record Management

Maintaining and categorizing digital records for easy retrieval under RTI requests.

Searchability

Implementing searchable databases to improve information discovery.

RTI Online Portal Guidelines

The National Informatics Centre (NIC) in collaboration with DoPT has developed the RTI Online Portal which enables citizens to file RTI applications and first appeals online. The following guidelines apply to the implementation and use of online RTI portals:

Aspect Guidelines
User Registration
  • Simple registration process requiring minimum information
  • Option to register using mobile number or email
  • No requirement for physical verification for basic registration
Application Filing
  • Text limit of 3000 characters for RTI application
  • Option to attach supporting documents (PDF only, up to 1MB)
  • Dropdown selection for concerned Public Authority
Fee Payment
  • Online payment options including net banking, UPI, and credit/debit cards
  • Automated exemption for BPL applicants (with proof upload)
  • Electronic receipt generation for all transactions
Tracking System
  • Unique Registration Number for tracking applications
  • SMS and email notifications at each stage of processing
  • Timeline display showing status of application/appeal
Reply Management
  • Digital delivery of responses to applicant's account
  • Option to download responses in PDF format
  • Facility to submit clarifications or first appeals online

Section 4 Digital Disclosure

Section 4(1)(b) of the RTI Act mandates proactive disclosure of information by public authorities. The DoPT has issued specific guidelines for digital implementation of Section 4 disclosures:

  • Dedicated RTI section accessible from the homepage
  • Standardized menu structure for Section 4 disclosures
  • Consistent naming conventions for RTI-related webpages
  • Bilingual content (English and Hindi/regional language)
  • Mobile-responsive design for all RTI webpages
Best Practice: Use a dedicated subdomain (e.g., rti.ministryname.gov.in) for easier navigation and management of RTI content.

  • Use open formats like PDF/A, ODF, CSV, and XML
  • Ensure data is machine-readable where applicable
  • Tabular information should be provided in spreadsheet formats
  • Documents should be OCR-enabled when containing text
  • Avoid proprietary formats that require specific software
Important: Image scans of documents are not considered fully compliant unless accompanied by OCR text or transcripts.

  • Compliance with WCAG 2.0 Level AA standards
  • Alt text for all images and graphics
  • Proper heading structure for screen reader navigation
  • Color contrast ratios that meet accessibility standards
  • Keyboard navigation support for all interactive elements
Note: All government websites must comply with GIGW (Guidelines for Indian Government Websites) which includes accessibility requirements.

  • Clear timestamp showing last update date
  • Quarterly mandatory review and update of Section 4 disclosures
  • Archive system for maintaining previous versions
  • Change log to document significant updates
  • Designated official responsible for content updates
Sample Update Statement:

"This information was last updated on [DATE] by [DESIGNATION]. Previous version available in archive section. Major changes in this update: [CHANGE LOG]."
