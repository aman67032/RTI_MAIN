RTI Filing Guide

Master the RTI application process with our comprehensive step-by-step guide

Your RTI Journey Starts Here

Filing an RTI application is a simple process, but understanding the correct approach can significantly increase your chances of receiving the information you seek. This guide walks you through each step of the RTI filing process with special focus on education-related applications.

Follow these steps carefully to ensure your application is processed efficiently and effectively.

1

Identify the Right Public Authority

Determine which public authority holds the information you're seeking. For education-related information, this could be:

  • School or university administration
  • Education department of your state
  • School boards (CBSE, ICSE, State Boards)
  • University Grants Commission (UGC)
  • Ministry of Education
  • Regulatory bodies like NCTE, AICTE, etc.

Pro Tip

If you're unsure about the correct authority, it's better to file with a higher authority (like the education department) rather than individual institutions. If your application reaches the wrong department, they are legally obligated to forward it to the right authority within 5 days.

2

Prepare Your Application

Your RTI application should include:

  • Your full name and contact details
  • Clear, specific questions about the information you seek
  • The relevant time period for the information
  • Declaration that you are an Indian citizen (if required)

Sample Format

To,
The Public Information Officer
[Name and Address of Public Authority]

Subject: Application under Right to Information Act, 2005

Sir/Madam,

I, [Your Name], would like to obtain the following information under the RTI Act, 2005:

1. [Specific Question 1]
2. [Specific Question 2]
3. [Specific Question 3]

The information relates to the period from [Start Date] to [End Date].

I am enclosing the application fee of Rs. 10/- by [payment method].

I declare that I am a citizen of India.

Yours faithfully,
[Your Name]
[Your Full Address]
[Your Contact Number]
[Date]

3

Pay the Application Fee

The standard RTI application fee is Rs. 10/-, which can be paid through various methods:

  • Indian Postal Order (IPO)
  • Demand Draft
  • Court Fee Stamp
  • Cash (if submitting in person)
  • Online payment (for some authorities)

Pro Tip

Indian Postal Orders (IPOs) are the most widely accepted payment method for RTI applications. They can be purchased from any post office.

Those living below the poverty line (BPL) are exempt from application fees. They need to attach a copy of their BPL certificate with the application.

4

Submit Your Application

You can submit your RTI application through various channels:

  • By Post: Send your application by registered post/speed post with acknowledgment due to maintain proof of delivery
  • In Person: Visit the public authority's office and submit your application at the RTI cell or to the designated PIO
  • Online: Use the RTI Online Portal (https://rtionline.gov.in/) for central government authorities
  • State RTI Portals: Many states have their own online RTI portals for state government departments

Pro Tip

Always keep a copy of your RTI application and proof of submission (receipt, postal tracking number, or acknowledgment) for future reference and follow-up.

5

Track Your Application

After submitting your application:

  • You should receive a response within 30 days (or 48 hours for matters concerning life and liberty)
  • Note the unique application number provided for future reference
  • If using postal methods, track delivery through the postal department's tracking system
  • For online applications, use the portal's tracking feature

6

Handle the Response

Once you receive a response, review it carefully:

  • If the information is complete and satisfactory, your RTI journey is successful
  • If the information is incomplete or denied, note the reasons provided
  • If no response is received within 30 days, it's considered a deemed refusal
  • Additional fees may be charged for providing information (Rs. 2 per page for photocopies)

Pro Tip

When additional fees are requested, you must pay them within 30 days. The 30-day response timeline is paused during this payment period and resumes once payment is made.

7

File an Appeal (If Necessary)

If your application is rejected or you receive incomplete information, you can file appeals:

  1. First Appeal: File with the First Appellate Authority (usually an officer senior to the PIO) within 30 days of receiving the response or within 30 days after the deadline if no response is received
  2. Second Appeal: If dissatisfied with the first appeal decision, file with the State/Central Information Commission within 90 days of receiving the first appeal decision

Sample First Appeal Format

To,
The First Appellate Authority
[Name and Address of the Authority]

Subject: First Appeal under Section 19(1) of RTI Act, 2005

Sir/Madam,

I had filed an RTI application dated [date] with RTI application number [number] with the PIO of your department. However:

[Select appropriate reason]
- I have not received any response within the stipulated time of 30 days
- The information provided is incomplete/incorrect
- The information has been wrongfully denied
- Other reason (specify)

I am therefore filing this first appeal. A copy of my original RTI application and the response received (if any) is enclosed.

I request you to direct the PIO to provide the information sought by me.

Yours faithfully,
[Your Name]
[Your Full Address]
[Your Contact Number]
[Date]

Common Mistakes to Avoid

Asking Vague Questions

Be specific about the information you seek. Avoid general questions like "Give me all information about admissions." Instead, ask "Provide the complete admission criteria and selection process for the 2024-25 academic year."

Requesting Opinions

RTI is for obtaining existing information, not opinions or explanations. Ask "Provide a copy of the fee structure," not "Why are the fees so high?"

Too Many Questions

Limit your application to relevant questions. Too many questions may lead to delays or rejection for being too voluminous.

Wrong Public Authority

Research to identify the correct authority holding the information you seek. Sending applications to wrong departments causes delays.

Missing Contact Details

Always include your complete postal address, contact number, and email for communication purposes.

Not Following Up

If no response is received within 30 days, follow up immediately. Don't wait too long to file appeals when needed.
