What is RTI?

The Right to Information Act (RTI) is a law enacted by the Parliament of India which sets out the rules and procedures regarding citizens' right to information. It replaced the former Freedom of Information Act, 2002.

Under the provisions of the Act, any citizen of India may request information from a "public authority" which is required to reply expeditiously or within thirty days. This law empowers students to access information about educational institutions, examination processes, and more.

Frequently Asked Questions

Common queries about RTI applications for students

What information can I request through RTI?

As a student, you can request information about examination results, answer sheets, evaluation criteria, admission processes, scholarship details, faculty qualifications, infrastructure facilities, and many other aspects of educational institutions.

How long does it take to get a response?

Public authorities are required to provide information within 30 days of receiving your application. In cases where the information sought concerns the life or liberty of a person, it must be provided within 48 hours.

What if I don't get a response or am not satisfied?

If you don't receive a response within 30 days, or if you're dissatisfied with the response, you can file a first appeal with the First Appellate Authority. If still unsatisfied, you can approach the Information Commission with a second appeal.

Can I ask questions or seek opinions through RTI?

The RTI Act allows you to seek information that exists in records held by public authorities. You cannot ask questions that require creating new information, providing opinions, or solving your problems.

Can private educational institutions be approached through RTI?

Private educational institutions not substantially financed by the government are not directly covered under the RTI Act. However, you can approach the regulatory bodies that govern these institutions, such as UGC, AICTE, or state education departments.

RTI Tips for Students

Making your RTI application effective can increase your chances of getting the information you need.

Be Specific

Ask for specific information rather than vague requests. Instead of asking "Why was I denied admission?", ask "What was the cutoff score for admission in my category?"

One Subject Per RTI

Focus on one subject matter per application to avoid rejection on grounds of being too broad or unclear.

Best Practices

  • Keep your questions clear and concise
  • Request information in easily accessible formats
  • Maintain copies of all correspondence
  • Follow up if you don't receive a response
