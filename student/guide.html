1

Identify the Public Authority

Determine which public authority holds the information you're seeking. This could be:

  • Your school or university
  • State education board
  • UGC (University Grants Commission)
  • CBSE, ICSE, or other examination boards
  • Ministry of Education
2

Draft Your RTI Application

Create a clear, concise application that includes:

  • Your personal details (name, contact info)
  • Specific questions about the information you seek
  • Relevant time period or context
  • Declaration that you are an Indian citizen
3

Pay the RTI Fee

Note: BPL cardholders are exempted from paying the fee.

4

Submit Your Application

Submit your RTI application through one of these methods:

  • Online through RTI Portal
  • By post to the PIO of the concerned authority
  • In person at the RTI cell of the authority
5

Track Your Application

Keep track of your RTI application:

  • Note down the RTI reference number
  • Expect a response within 30 days
  • Follow up if you don't receive a response
  • File an appeal if information is denied or incomplete
