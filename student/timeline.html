RTI Timeline

The evolution of information rights in India through key events and judgments

December 1982

S.P. Gupta v. Union of India

The Supreme Court recognized that the right to information is implicit in the right to freedom of speech and expression under Article 19(1)(a) of the Constitution. This landmark judgment, often referred to as the "First Judges Case," established that disclosure of information regarding the functioning of government is the rule and secrecy is an exception.

1990

Mazdoor Kisan Shakti Sangathan Movement

The grassroots movement in Rajasthan led by Aruna Roy and others began demanding access to official records related to development works. This movement laid the foundation for the RTI movement in India and demonstrated the power of information in fighting corruption.

1997

Tamil Nadu RTI Act

Tamil Nadu became the first state in India to enact a Right to Information law at the state level. This pioneering legislation provided a framework for citizens to access information from state government departments.

2000

Rajasthan RTI Act

Following grassroots activism by MKSS, Rajasthan passed its own RTI Act, which was considered one of the more progressive state-level RTI laws and included provisions for penalties for non-compliance.

2002

Freedom of Information Act

The Parliament passed the Freedom of Information Act, 2002, the first national-level legislation on the right to information. However, this Act was never effectively implemented as the necessary rules were not notified.

2002

Association for Democratic Reforms Case

The Supreme Court ruled that voters have the right to know the antecedents of candidates contesting elections, including their criminal records, assets, and educational qualifications, establishing the right to information as essential for democratic participation.

June 15, 2005

RTI Act Passed

The Right to Information Act, 2005 was passed by the Parliament, replacing the ineffective Freedom of Information Act, 2002. The RTI Act provided a comprehensive framework for citizens to access information from public authorities at all levels of government.

October 12, 2005

RTI Act Comes into Force

The Right to Information Act, 2005 came into force, marking a significant milestone in India's journey towards transparency and accountability in governance. The Act established Information Commissions at the central and state levels and mandated the appointment of Public Information Officers in all public authorities.

2007

Establishment of CIC and SICs

The Central Information Commission (CIC) and State Information Commissions (SICs) were established and became operational, providing the institutional framework for implementing the RTI Act and hearing appeals against denials of information.

2008

Namit Sharma v. Union of India

The Supreme Court issued guidelines regarding the qualifications and appointment process for Information Commissioners, emphasizing the need for judicial members in Information Commissions. This judgment was later partially modified in 2013.

September 13, 2011

CBSE v. Aditya Bandopadhyay

The Supreme Court held that evaluated answer sheets of students in board examinations are "information" under the RTI Act and can be accessed by the students. This judgment expanded the scope of "information" under the RTI Act to include examination records.

2012

Girish Ramchandra Deshpande Case

The Supreme Court ruled on the balance between transparency and privacy, holding that personal information of public servants is exempt from disclosure unless there is a larger public interest. This judgment has been widely cited in cases involving privacy exemptions under the RTI Act.

November 13, 2013

CJI's Office Under RTI

The Delhi High Court ruled that the office of the Chief Justice of India comes under the purview of the RTI Act, enhancing judicial transparency. The Court held that judicial independence and transparency are not in conflict but complement each other.

December 16, 2015

RBI v. Jayantilal N. Mistry

The Supreme Court held that the RBI cannot refuse to disclose information about banks under its supervision by claiming exemptions under the RTI Act. This judgment established that regulatory bodies cannot shield information about regulated entities by claiming exemptions.

2016

Lok Sabha Secretariat v. CIC

The Delhi High Court ruled that the Lok Sabha Secretariat is a public authority under the RTI Act, reinforcing that parliamentary bodies are subject to transparency requirements despite their constitutional status.

2018

Sarbajit Roy v. SEBI

The Central Information Commission ruled that stock exchanges (BSE and NSE) are public authorities under the RTI Act, expanding the scope of the Act to include market infrastructure institutions that perform public functions.

July 25, 2019

RTI Amendment Act

The Right to Information (Amendment) Act, 2019 was passed, modifying provisions related to the terms and conditions of service of Information Commissioners. The amendments gave the central government the power to determine the tenure, salaries, and conditions of service of Information Commissioners at both central and state levels.

November 13, 2019

Supreme Court on CJI's Office

The Supreme Court upheld the Delhi High Court's decision that the office of the Chief Justice of India comes under the RTI Act. However, the Court emphasized that disclosure would be subject to considerations of judicial independence, privacy, and confidentiality.

October 2020

RTI During COVID-19

The Central Information Commission directed the government to proactively disclose information related to the PM CARES Fund, established for COVID-19 relief. This highlighted the importance of transparency even during emergency situations.

2021

Anjali Bhardwaj v. Union of India

The Supreme Court directed the Central and State governments to fill vacancies in Information Commissions in a timely manner, emphasizing that the proper functioning of these commissions is essential for the effective implementation of the RTI Act.

March 2022

Digital RTI Portal Enhancement

The government launched enhanced features for the online RTI portal, making it more user-friendly and accessible. The new features included improved tracking of applications, multilingual support, and integration with mobile platforms.

2023

15 Years of RTI Implementation Report

Civil society organizations released comprehensive reports assessing 15+ years of RTI implementation, highlighting both achievements and challenges. The reports showed that while the RTI Act has empowered citizens, issues like backlogs, vacancies, and attempts to dilute the law remain concerns.

July 2023

Supreme Court on Proactive Disclosure

The Supreme Court issued a landmark judgment emphasizing the importance of proactive disclosure under Section 4 of the RTI Act. The Court directed all public authorities to strengthen their proactive disclosure mechanisms and make information available in digital formats to reduce the need for formal RTI applications.

October 2023

National RTI Awards Instituted

The government instituted the National RTI Awards to recognize outstanding contributions by citizens, civil society organizations, and public authorities in promoting transparency and effective implementation of the RTI Act. The awards aimed to incentivize best practices and innovative approaches to information disclosure.

January 2024

RTI 2.0 Initiative Launched

The government launched the "RTI 2.0" initiative to modernize the RTI framework using emerging technologies. The initiative included blockchain-based record-keeping, AI-powered information retrieval systems, and enhanced digital platforms for filing and tracking RTI applications across all public authorities.

April 2024

RTI Mobile App Launch

The Central Information Commission launched a comprehensive mobile application for RTI, allowing citizens to file applications, track status, and receive responses directly on their smartphones. The app included features like voice-based filing, regional language support, and integration with digital payment systems for application fees.

August 2024

Supreme Court on Political Parties under RTI

In a significant judgment, the Supreme Court ruled that political parties fall within the definition of "public authorities" under the RTI Act, making them subject to transparency requirements. The Court directed all national and regional political parties to appoint Public Information Officers and comply with the provisions of the RTI Act.

November 2024

RTI Amendment Bill 2024

The government introduced the RTI Amendment Bill 2024 in Parliament, proposing comprehensive reforms to strengthen the RTI framework. The bill included provisions for time-bound disposal of appeals, protection for whistleblowers, mandatory training for Public Information Officers, and enhanced penalties for non-compliance.

January 2025

National RTI Portal Integration

The government completed the integration of all state RTI portals with the National RTI Portal, creating a unified platform for citizens to file RTI applications to any public authority across the country. The integrated portal featured a standardized interface, centralized tracking, and comprehensive analytics on RTI implementation.

February 15, 2025

Global Transparency Index Recognition

India's RTI framework received international recognition in the Global Transparency Index, with significant improvement in rankings due to recent reforms and digital initiatives. The index highlighted India's innovative approaches to information disclosure and citizen engagement in governance through the RTI mechanism.

March 12, 2025

RTI Implementation in AI Governance

The government issued comprehensive guidelines on applying RTI principles to AI systems used in public administration. The guidelines mandated transparency in algorithmic decision-making, disclosure of AI training data sources, and citizen access to information about how AI systems affect public service delivery and policy implementation.
