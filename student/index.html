Your Rights as a Student

  • Right to access educational records and information about evaluation criteria
  • Right to know about admission processes and fee structures
  • Right to transparency in examination and evaluation processes
  • Right to information about scholarships and financial aid
  • Right to know about faculty qualifications and infrastructure facilities
  • Right to fair treatment and non-discrimination
  • Right to participate in decision-making processes affecting student welfare

Constitutional Framework

Article 21A
Right to Education as a Fundamental Right - Guarantees free and compulsory education to all children between 6-14 years.
Article 29(2)
Protection against discrimination in admission to educational institutions maintained by the State or receiving aid from the State.
Article 30
Right of minorities to establish and administer educational institutions of their choice.
RTI Act, 2005
Empowers students to seek information from public educational institutions about their functioning, policies, and decisions.

The Right to Information Act empowers you to request information from various public authorities across India.

What are Public Authorities?

Public authorities include government bodies, departments, institutions, and organizations that are substantially financed by government funds.

Filing RTI for Education

As a student, you can file RTIs to inquire about examination processes, admission criteria, scholarship disbursement, infrastructure facilities, and more.

Tips for Filing Effective RTIs

  • Be specific in your questions
  • Address the correct public authority
  • Limit to one subject matter per application
  • Keep track of your application status
