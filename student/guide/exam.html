Examination RTI Guide

How to file RTIs for examination-related information

Request copies of your evaluated answer sheets

Get detailed marking schemes used for evaluation

Result Statistics

Statistical data related to exam results

Step-by-Step Process

1

Identify the Correct Authority

Determine which educational institution or examination body holds your information.

For university exams, file RTI with the university's Public Information Officer. For national exams like JEE, NEET, etc., file with the conducting body (NTA, CBSE, etc.)

2

Frame Specific Questions

Be precise about what examination information you're seeking.

Sample Questions:

  • "Please provide a certified copy of my answer sheet for [Exam Name] held on [Date]."
  • "Please provide the detailed marking scheme used for evaluating [Subject/Paper] in [Exam]."
  • "Please provide information about the qualification and experience of examiners who evaluated [Subject/Paper]."
3

Submit Application

File your RTI application with the appropriate fee.

For most educational institutions, the RTI fee is ₹10. You can pay via postal order, demand draft, or online (if available).

Go to RTI Form
4

Track Your Application

Monitor the status of your RTI application.

Legal Timeline

30 Days

Information Response

The public authority must provide the requested information within 30 days of receiving your application.

45 Days

Information Involving Third Party

If the information involves a third party, the time limit is extended to 45 days.

48 Hours

Life or Liberty Matters

If the information concerns the life or liberty of a person, it must be provided within 48 hours.

Important Supreme Court Judgments

CBSE & Anr. vs. Aditya Bandopadhyay & Ors. (2011)

The Supreme Court ruled that evaluated answer sheets are "information" under the RTI Act and students have the right to access their evaluated answer sheets.

University of Calcutta vs. Pritam Rooj (2020)

The Supreme Court held that the procedure prescribed for accessing evaluated answer sheets cannot override the provisions of the RTI Act.
