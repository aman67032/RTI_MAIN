Admission RTI Guide

How to file RTIs for admission-related information

What Information Can You Request?

Selection Criteria

Details about admission criteria and evaluation process

Seat Matrix

Distribution of seats across categories and departments

Cut-off Marks

Category-wise cut-off marks for previous years

Fee Structure

Detailed breakdown of fees for different courses

Common Admission RTI Scenarios

Admission Rejection

If your admission application was rejected despite meeting eligibility criteria, you can file an RTI to get detailed information about the selection process.

Sample Question:

"Please provide the complete details of the selection process for [Course Name] for the academic year [Year], including the cutoff marks/rank for my category, and specific reasons for rejection of my application (Application No: [Your Number])."

Waitlist Status

If you're on a waitlist and want to know your chances of admission, you can request information about the waitlist movement.

Sample Question:

"Please provide information about my current position on the waitlist for [Course Name] (Application No: [Your Number]), the number of students already admitted, and the number of vacant seats still available in my category."

Reservation Implementation

To verify proper implementation of reservation policies, you can request category-wise seat allocation details.

Sample Question:

"Please provide the complete category-wise seat matrix for [Course Name] for the academic year [Year], showing the number of seats reserved under different categories and the actual number of students admitted under each category."

Step-by-Step Process

1

Identify the Correct Authority

Determine which educational institution or admission body to target.

For college/university admissions, file with the institution's Public Information Officer. For centralized admissions (like JEE counseling), file with the conducting body (JoSAA, CSAB, etc.).

2

Frame Specific Questions

Be precise about what admission information you're seeking.

Be Specific:

  • Include your application number, course name, and academic year
  • Ask for specific documents like seat matrices, cut-off lists, or selection criteria
  • If questioning rejection, clearly ask for the specific reasons
3

Submit Application

File your RTI application with the appropriate fee.

For educational institutions, the RTI fee is ₹10. You may need to pay additional charges if you request a large number of documents.

Go to RTI Form
4

Track Your Application

Monitor the status of your RTI application.

Important Legal Precedents

CBSE v. Aditya Bandopadhyay (2011)

The Supreme Court ruled that educational institutions must disclose their evaluation criteria and methods of admission to ensure transparency.

Mohnish Mohan Mukkar v. Union of India (2015)

The Delhi High Court ruled that entrance examination bodies must disclose answer keys and question papers under the RTI Act.

UPSC v. Angesh Kumar (2018)

The Supreme Court held that while detailed procedures of selection can be disclosed, information that would compromise the competitive examination system need not be disclosed.
