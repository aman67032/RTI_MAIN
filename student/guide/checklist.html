RTI Application Checklist

Follow this step-by-step checklist to ensure your RTI application is complete and effective

0% Complete (0/15 items)

Before Filing

Identify the Correct Public Authority

Determine which educational institution or government body holds the information you need.

For college admissions, target the specific college. For entrance exams, target the conducting body (e.g., NTA for JEE/NEET).

Research Existing Information

Check if the information you're seeking is already available on the institution's website or other public sources.

Many institutions publish information under Section 4 of the RTI Act on their websites. Check for "RTI Disclosures" or "Mandatory Disclosures" sections.

Clarify Your Information Needs

Be clear about what specific information you need and why it's important for you as a student.

Make a list of specific data points or documents you need. Vague questions often lead to unhelpful responses.

Preparing Your Application

Frame Specific Questions

Draft clear, precise questions that specify exactly what information you need.

Instead of asking "Tell me about admissions," ask "Please provide the category-wise cutoff marks for BTech Computer Science for the 2022-23 academic year."

Focus on One Subject Per Application

Keep your RTI application focused on a single subject matter to avoid rejection.

File separate RTIs for unrelated topics. For example, don't mix questions about admissions and scholarships in one application.

Limit the Number of Questions

Avoid overwhelming the PIO with too many questions in a single application.

Aim for 5-7 specific questions per application. Too many questions may lead to rejection due to "voluminous information."

Include Relevant References

Mention relevant details like application numbers, roll numbers, or specific time periods.

For example: "Regarding Application No. XYZ123 submitted on [Date] for admission to [Course]."

Filing Your Application

Use the Correct Format

Use the standard RTI application format or the specific format required by the public authority.

Use our templates section for pre-formatted RTI applications tailored for student needs.

Provide Your Contact Information

Include your complete name, address, phone number, and email for communication.

Make sure your contact information is legible and current. This is how the PIO will reach you with their response.

Pay the Required Fee

Include the application fee of ₹10 in the appropriate form.

For online applications, pay via online gateway. For physical applications, use postal order, demand draft, or court fee stamp as accepted by the authority.

Sign Your Application

Don't forget to sign your RTI application before sending it.

For online applications, digital signatures or checking the verification box is sufficient. For physical applications, a handwritten signature is required.

After Filing

Keep a Copy of Your Application

Maintain a copy of your RTI application and any acknowledgment received.

Take a screenshot or save a PDF of online applications. Keep a photocopy of physical applications along with posting receipt.

Track Your Application

Monitor the status of your RTI application using the provided registration number.

Use our Track Status feature to monitor your application's progress. For online applications, most portals provide a tracking facility.

Be Prepared for Additional Fees

The PIO may request additional fees for providing voluminous information.

If asked to pay additional fees, respond promptly. The time between the fee request and your payment is not counted in the 30-day timeline.

Know Your Appeal Rights

Be aware of the appeal process if you don't receive a satisfactory response.

You can file a First Appeal within 30 days if information is denied or no response is received. Second Appeal can be filed with the Information Commission within 90 days of First Appeal decision.
