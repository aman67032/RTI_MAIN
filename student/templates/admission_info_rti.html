Admission Information RTI Template

Request transparency in educational institution admission processes

When to Use This Template

This template is designed for students seeking information about admission processes in educational institutions. Use this template when:

  • You need information about admission criteria and selection procedures
  • You want to understand how seats are allocated, including reservation policies
  • You need clarity on cutoff marks or ranking methodology
  • You want information about management quotas and their allocation
  • You need details about fees structures and their components
  • You want to understand the waitlist process and vacant seat allocation

Key Points to Remember

  1. Be specific: Clearly mention the course, year, and specific aspects of the admission process you want information about.
  2. Request documents: Ask for certified copies of official notifications, brochures, or government orders related to the admission process.
  3. Avoid personal information: Your RTI cannot request information about other students' personal details.
  4. Focus on process: Ask about the process, criteria, and methodology rather than specific decisions about individuals.
  5. Mention time period: Specify the academic year or admission cycle you are inquiring about.
  6. Follow up: If you receive an incomplete response, you can file a first appeal within 30 days.

Success Stories

VM

Vikram Malhotra

Engineering Aspirant

I was denied admission to a prestigious engineering college despite having a higher rank than many who were admitted. I filed an RTI seeking information about the selection criteria and discovered that the college had a hidden 'domicile quota' that wasn't mentioned in their prospectus. I appealed to the admission committee with this information and was granted admission in the same academic year.

Result: Secured admission after exposing undisclosed quota system
SP

Sneha Patil

Medical College Applicant

When applying to a private medical college, I was asked to pay a 'development fee' of ₹5 lakhs in addition to the regular fees. Using the RTI template, I requested information about the fee structure approved by the state fee regulatory committee. The response revealed that the college was charging ₹3 lakhs more than the approved amount. I reported this to the state education department, which led to a reduction in fees not just for me but for all students.

Result: ₹3 lakh fee reduction for all incoming students
AR

Akash Raj

MBA Aspirant

I was on the waitlist for an MBA program and received no communication despite seeing new admissions happening. Through an RTI, I discovered that the institution was admitting students with lower scores through a 'management quota' without exhausting the waitlist first, which violated their own published admission policy. With this information, I approached the university ombudsman who directed the institute to follow proper waitlist procedures, resulting in my admission.

Result: Policy violation exposed, waitlist properly implemented
