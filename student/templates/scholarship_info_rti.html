Scholarship Information RTI Template

Request transparency about scholarships, grants, and financial aid programs

When to Use This Template

This template is designed for students seeking detailed information about scholarships and financial aid offered by educational institutions and government bodies. Use this template when:

  • You need information about eligibility criteria for scholarships
  • You want details about scholarship disbursement procedures
  • You need information about the total number of scholarships allocated and granted
  • You want to know about scholarship funds utilization and distribution
  • You're concerned about delays in scholarship disbursement
  • You need details about special category scholarships (SC/ST/OBC/Minority/Disabled)
  • You want information about merit-based vs. need-based scholarship distributions
  • You need to verify the selection process and criteria for awarding scholarships

Sample Specific Information Request

I am requesting the following specific information regarding scholarships at your institution/organization:

  1. A comprehensive list of all scholarships, grants, and financial aid programs available to students for the academic year [YEAR], including:
    • Name and type of each scholarship (merit-based, need-based, category-based, etc.)
    • The amount/value of each scholarship
    • Number of seats/positions available for each scholarship
  2. Detailed eligibility criteria for each scholarship program, including:
    • Academic requirements (minimum grades/percentages required)
    • Income limits for need-based scholarships
    • Category requirements (SC/ST/OBC/Minority/Disabled quotas)
    • Any additional criteria or qualifications needed
  3. Statistical data regarding scholarships for the last academic year [PREVIOUS YEAR], including:
    • Total number of applications received for each scholarship
    • Number of scholarships granted, by category and department
    • Total amount disbursed in scholarships
    • Number of applications rejected and grounds for rejection
  4. Information about the disbursement process, including:
    • Timeline from application to disbursement
    • Method of fund transfer to students
    • Any installment schedules for multi-year scholarships
    • Authorities responsible for approving and releasing scholarship funds
  5. Details about any unclaimed scholarship funds:
    • Amount of unclaimed scholarship funds in the previous academic year
    • Reasons for non-disbursement of allocated funds
    • How unclaimed funds are utilized or reallocated
  6. Information about any special scholarship schemes:
    • Details of scholarships specifically for women in STEM fields
    • Scholarships for first-generation college students
    • Any new scholarship programs introduced in the last two years

Key Points to Remember

  1. Be specific about the scholarship: If you're inquiring about a particular scholarship, mention its exact name and academic year.
  2. Request numerical data: Ask for specific numbers - how many students applied, how many received scholarships, total amount disbursed, etc.
  3. Inquire about selection criteria: Request information about how recipients are selected and the weightage given to different factors.
  4. Ask about timelines: Request information about application deadlines, announcement dates, and disbursement schedules.
  5. Seek category-wise breakdowns: Ask for data broken down by categories like SC/ST/OBC/General or by department/course.
  6. Inquire about unclaimed funds: Ask about scholarships that went unclaimed and how those funds were utilized.

Engineering Student

Our university claimed that all eligible SC/ST students received their scholarships, but many of my classmates hadn't received their funds for over 8 months. I filed an RTI requesting information about the disbursement status of SC/ST scholarships. The response revealed that while the government had released the funds to the university 6 months earlier, the university had not processed them due to "administrative reasons." Within two weeks of my RTI findings being shared with the student welfare committee, the university disbursed all pending scholarships to over 200 students.

PC

Priya Chaudhary

Medical Student

I was confused about why I wasn't eligible for a state merit scholarship despite having higher marks than some recipients. Through an RTI application, I requested the complete selection criteria and the cut-off marks for each department. The response revealed that the medical college was using an outdated formula for calculating merit that wasn't aligned with the state government's current guidelines. Additionally, it showed that the scholarship committee had mistakenly applied the wrong cut-off scores for medical students. When this information was presented to the college authorities, they acknowledged the error and recalculated the merit list.

Result: Scholarship awarded retroactively to 35 deserving students who had been overlooked
