Interactive RTI Filing Guide

A step-by-step guide to help you file effective RTI applications for educational matters

1

Identify the Information You Need

The first step in filing an effective RTI application is to clearly identify what information you need. Being specific helps you get relevant answers faster.

Key Actions:

  • Define your objective: What problem are you trying to solve?
  • Be specific about the documents or information you need
  • Determine the time period for which you need information
  • Break down complex queries into simple, direct questions

Examples of Specific Information Requests:

  • Instead of "Give me information about admissions," ask for "Complete admission criteria and weightage for Class XI Science stream for the academic year 2024-25"
  • Instead of "Tell me about school fees," ask for "Detailed breakdown of fee structure for Class VIII, including all components, for the academic year 2024-25"
2

Find the Right Public Authority

Identify the correct public authority that holds the information you're seeking. This ensures your application reaches the right department without delays.

Key Actions:

  • Research which educational institution or department would have the information
  • Identify the Public Information Officer (PIO) of that institution
  • Check the official website of the institution for RTI details
  • For schools, determine if they fall under RTI Act (government/aided schools do; many private schools don't unless they receive government aid)

Common Educational Authorities:

  • School-level information: The school's PIO or District Education Officer
  • Board exams/results: CBSE, ICSE, State Education Boards
  • Higher education: University Registrar, UGC, AICTE
  • Scholarships/funds: Ministry of Education, Department of School Education & Literacy
3

Draft Your RTI Application

A well-drafted RTI application is clear, concise, and follows the prescribed format. This helps in quick processing and comprehensive responses.

Key Components:

  1. Addressee: The PIO of the relevant institution
  2. Applicant details: Your name, address, contact information
  3. Subject line: Clearly stating it's an RTI application
  4. Information requested: Clear, numbered points of information you seek
  5. Time period: Specify the academic year or date range
  6. Application fee statement: Mention you're enclosing the required fee
  7. Declaration: You're an Indian citizen (required for RTI Act)
  8. Date and signature

Tips for Effective Drafting:

  • Use simple, straightforward language
  • Ask specific questions rather than making general requests
  • Number your questions for easy reference
  • Stick to 10 or fewer questions per application for best results
  • Avoid asking for opinions; ask for documents and facts

Sample Templates:

  • Admission Process RTI Template
  • Fee Structure RTI Template
  • Fund Tracking RTI Template
4

Submit Your Application

Properly submitting your RTI application with the correct fee and through the right channel ensures it gets processed without delays.

Submission Methods:

  1. In-person: Visit the office of the public authority and submit directly
  2. By post: Send by registered post/speed post for proof of delivery
  3. Online: Use the RTI Online Portal (https://rtionline.gov.in/) for central government authorities
  4. State RTI portals: Many states have their own online RTI submission systems

Application Fee:

  • Central Government: ₹10
  • State Governments: Varies by state (typically ₹10-50)
  • Payment methods:
    • Cash (when submitting in person)
    • Postal order/Demand Draft
    • Court fee stamp
    • Online payment (for online submissions)
  • Fee exemption: BPL (Below Poverty Line) category citizens are exempt from paying the application fee

What to Include:

  • The RTI application (original plus one copy for your records)
  • Application fee
  • Self-addressed envelope (if applying by post)
  • Cover letter requesting acknowledgment
5

Track and Follow Up

After submitting your RTI application, it's important to track its progress and follow up appropriately to ensure you receive a timely response.

Timeline to Remember:

  • 30 days: Maximum time for the PIO to respond to your application
  • 48 hours: When information concerns life or liberty of a person
  • 35 days: When application is transferred to another public authority
  • 40 days: When third party information is involved

Tracking Methods:

  • Keep your application reference number or acknowledgment receipt safe
  • For postal applications, use tracking services to confirm delivery
  • For online applications, use the portal's tracking feature
  • Call the PIO office after 15-20 days to inquire about status

Follow-up Actions:

  • If no response after 30 days, prepare for first appeal
  • If information is incomplete, write to the PIO asking for complete information
  • If additional fee is requested, pay promptly to avoid delays
  • Keep records of all communications
6

Appeal Process

If your RTI application is denied, provides incomplete information, or if you receive no response within 30 days, you have the right to appeal.

Two-Level Appeal System:

  1. First Appeal:
    • Filed with the First Appellate Authority (senior officer to the PIO)
    • Must be filed within 30 days of receiving response or the 30-day deadline
    • No fee required for first appeal
    • Decision should come within 30-45 days
  2. Second Appeal:
    • Filed with the Central/State Information Commission
    • Must be filed within 90 days of first appeal decision or deadline
    • More formal process, may involve hearings
    • Commission has powers to impose penalties on PIOs

Drafting an Appeal:

  • Include original RTI application details and reference numbers
  • Clearly state grounds for appeal (no response, incomplete information, etc.)
  • Attach copies of all previous correspondence
  • Specify the relief sought (what information you still need)

Tips for Successful Appeals:

  • Be factual and avoid emotional arguments
  • Focus on how the PIO failed to fulfill RTI Act obligations
  • Highlight any relevant court judgments or previous Information Commission orders
  • For education-related matters, emphasize the public interest in transparency
