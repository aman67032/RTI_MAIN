RTI Terminology & Definitions

This glossary provides definitions of key terms used in the RTI Act and related processes. Understanding these terms is essential for effectively using the Right to Information.
A B C D E F G I J N O P R S T

A

Appellant
A person who makes an appeal to the First Appellate Authority or to the Information Commission against the decision of the PIO.
Appellate Authority (First Appellate Authority - FAA)
An officer senior in rank to the Public Information Officer designated by the public authority to hear first appeals against the decisions of the PIO.
Application Fee
The fee that is required to be paid along with the RTI application. Currently set at â‚¹10 for central government bodies, though it may vary for state government bodies.

B

Below Poverty Line (BPL)
Persons belonging to the Below Poverty Line category are exempt from paying application fee and additional fees for obtaining information under the RTI Act.

C

Central Information Commission (CIC)
A statutory body established under the RTI Act to hear appeals against information denials by Central Public Authorities, and to oversee and guide the implementation of the Act.
Chief Information Commissioner
The head of the Central Information Commission or State Information Commission who directs and manages the affairs of the commission.
Competent Authority
As defined in Section 2(e) of the RTI Act, includes the Speaker of Lok Sabha, Chairman of Rajya Sabha, Chief Justice of India, Chief Justices of High Courts, and other constitutional authorities who can make rules under the Act.
Complaint
A grievance filed with the Information Commission under Section 18 of the RTI Act regarding certain violations or non-implementation of the Act.

D

Deemed PIO
An officer who is not designated as a PIO but assists the PIO in providing information, and can be held liable under the Act as per Section 5(5).
Deemed Refusal
When no response is provided to an RTI application within the stipulated time period (30 days), it is considered as a deemed refusal of information.
Disclosure
The act of making information available to the applicant under the provisions of the RTI Act.

E

Exemption
Categories of information that are exempt from disclosure under Section 8 of the RTI Act, such as information that would prejudicially affect national security, commercial confidence, or personal privacy.

F

Fee
The amount charged for processing an RTI application and providing information. This includes the application fee and additional fees for photocopying, inspection, etc.
First Appeal
An appeal filed with the First Appellate Authority when an applicant is dissatisfied with the response (or non-response) of the PIO.

G

Good Faith
As per Section 21 of the RTI Act, no suit or legal proceeding can be initiated against any person for anything done in good faith under the Act or rules made thereunder.

I

Information
As defined in Section 2(f), information means any material in any form, including records, documents, memos, e-mails, opinions, advices, press releases, circulars, orders, logbooks, contracts, reports, papers, samples, models, data material held in any electronic form.
Information Commissioner
A member of the Central Information Commission or State Information Commission who adjudicates on matters related to appeals and complaints under the RTI Act.
Inspection
The process where an applicant examines records or documents in person at the office of the public authority, as permitted under the RTI Act.

J

Judicial Independence
A principle recognized in the RTI Act that protects certain judicial information from disclosure to maintain the independence of the judiciary.

N

NODAL Agency
The Department of Personnel and Training (DoPT) is the nodal agency for implementation of the RTI Act at the central level.
Non-compliance
Failure to comply with the provisions of the RTI Act, which may result in penalties under Section 20 of the Act.

O

Official Secrets Act
A law that restricts access to certain government information. Section 22 of the RTI Act gives it overriding effect over the Official Secrets Act.

P

Penalty
Punishment imposed on a PIO under Section 20 of the RTI Act for refusing to receive an application, delaying information, providing incorrect information, or destroying information. The penalty can be up to â‚¹250 per day up to a maximum of â‚¹25,000.
Prescribed
As defined in Section 2(g), prescribed means prescribed by rules made under the RTI Act by the appropriate Government or the competent authority.
Proactive Disclosure
Information that public authorities are required to publish suo moto (on their own) under Section 4 of the RTI Act without waiting for specific requests.
Public Authority
As defined in Section 2(h), any authority or body or institution of self-government established or constituted by or under the Constitution; by any other law made by Parliament or State Legislature; by notification issued or order made by the appropriate Government, and includes bodies owned, controlled or substantially financed by the Government.
Public Information Officer (PIO)
An officer designated by a public authority under Section 5(1) of the RTI Act to provide information to persons requesting information under the Act.

R

Record
As defined in Section 2(i), includes any document, manuscript and file; any microfilm, microfiche and facsimile copy of a document; any reproduction of image(s) embodied in such microfilm; and any other material produced by a computer or any other device.
Right to Information
As defined in Section 2(j), the right to information accessible under the RTI Act which is held by or under the control of any public authority and includes the right to inspection, taking notes, extracts, certified copies, and accessing information in digital form.

S

Second Appeal
An appeal filed with the Information Commission when an applicant is dissatisfied with the decision of the First Appellate Authority.
Severability
The principle under Section 10 of the RTI Act that allows access to part of a record that does not contain exempt information, by severing (removing) the exempt portions.
State Information Commission (SIC)
A statutory body established under the RTI Act at the state level to hear appeals against information denials by State Public Authorities, and to oversee and guide the implementation of the Act in the state.

T

Third Party
As defined in Section 2(n), a person other than the citizen making a request for information and includes a public authority. The Act provides for consultation with third parties when their information might be disclosed.
Time Limit
The period within which information must be provided under the RTI Act, generally 30 days from the date of receipt of the request, or 48 hours for information concerning life or liberty.
Transfer of Application
The process under Section 6(3) where a PIO transfers an RTI application or a part of it to another public authority when the information sought is held by that authority.

Did You Know?

The RTI Act covers more than 60,000 public authorities across India, making it one of the most extensive transparency laws in the world.

The term "substantially financed" in the definition of public authority has been interpreted by courts to mean direct or indirect financing of 20% or more.

Section 4 of the RTI Act which mandates proactive disclosure is one of the least implemented sections of the law.
