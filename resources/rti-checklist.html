Common Mistakes to Avoid

Vague Questions

Avoid broad, unclear questions like "Tell me everything about project X." Be specific about what information you need.

Too Many Questions

Asking numerous unrelated questions in one application may lead to incomplete responses or rejection. File separate RTIs if needed.

Asking for Opinions

RTI is for seeking factual information, not opinions or explanations. Avoid questions like "Why did you make this decision?"

Wrong Authority

Filing with the wrong public authority leads to transfers and delays. Research which authority holds the information you seek.

Expert Tip
Life & Liberty Provision

If your RTI request concerns the life or liberty of a person, mention this specifically in your application. Under Section 7(1) of the RTI Act, such information must be provided within 48 hours instead of the usual 30 days.

— RTI Activist, Shailesh Gandhi
