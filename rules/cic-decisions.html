About CIC Decisions

The Central Information Commission (CIC) is the apex body established under the Right to Information Act to hear appeals and complaints from information seekers. Its decisions serve as precedents for implementing the RTI Act across the country.

CIC decisions are binding on public authorities and significantly influence RTI implementation and interpretation.

Significance of CIC Decisions

The decisions of the CIC are important for several reasons:

  • They provide interpretation of provisions in the RTI Act
  • They establish precedents for handling similar cases
  • They evolve standards for disclosure of information
  • They clarify the scope and limitations of the right to information
  • They promote transparency and accountability in governance

Landmark CIC Decisions

Case: Subhash Chandra Agarwal vs. Indian National Congress & Others

Decision Date: June 3, 2013

Full Decision

Key Points:

  • The CIC declared six national political parties as "public authorities" under the RTI Act
  • Political parties receive substantial indirect funding from the government through tax exemptions and free land allotments
  • They perform public functions and have constitutional and statutory obligations
  • Therefore, they are obligated to disclose information under the RTI Act

Impact: This decision attempted to bring political parties under the purview of the RTI Act, though political parties have largely resisted compliance.

Case: Subhash Chandra Agarwal vs. Supreme Court of India

Decision Date: January 6, 2009

Full Decision

Key Points:

  • The CIC ruled that the Office of the Chief Justice of India comes under the purview of the RTI Act
  • It directed the Supreme Court to disclose information about judges' assets
  • The right to information is a fundamental right under Article 19(1)(a) of the Constitution
  • Transparency in judiciary is essential for maintaining public confidence

Impact: This decision led to greater transparency in the judiciary, with judges now disclosing their assets publicly.

Case: CBSE vs. Aditya Bandopadhyay

Decision Date: August 9, 2011

Full Decision

Key Points:

  • The Supreme Court upheld the CIC's decision that answer sheets are information under the RTI Act
  • Examining bodies like CBSE must provide evaluated answer sheets to students upon request
  • The court clarified that disclosure of answer sheets doesn't interfere with the examining body's functioning
  • It distinguished between the RTI process and the formal process for revaluation of answer sheets

Impact: This decision significantly improved transparency in educational assessment and empowered students to access their evaluated answer sheets.

Case: R.K. Jain vs. Union of India

Decision Date: February 13, 2012

Full Decision

Key Points:

  • The CIC ruled that cabinet papers must be disclosed after the decision-making process is completed
  • Section 8(1)(i) of the RTI Act provides only limited exemption for cabinet papers
  • The exemption ends once the decision is taken and the matter is complete
  • This promotes greater transparency in high-level government decision-making

Impact: This decision has led to greater transparency in executive decision-making and allowed citizens to understand the rationale behind policy decisions.
