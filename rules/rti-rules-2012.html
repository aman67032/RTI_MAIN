Overview of RTI Rules 2012

The Right to Information Rules, 2012 were formulated to regulate the procedure in the matters of appeal to the Central Information Commission under Section 19 of the Right to Information Act, 2005. These rules replaced the earlier Right to Information Rules, 2007.

These rules came into effect from 31st July, 2012 through a gazette notification by the Department of Personnel and Training.

Application Procedure

As per the RTI Rules 2012, the application procedure involves several key steps:

Every appeal filed under section 19 of the Act shall be accompanied by:

  • Self-attested copies of the orders against which the appeal is being preferred
  • Copies of documents relied upon by the appellant and referred to in the appeal
  • An index of the documents referred to in the appeal

The Rules prescribe the following fee structure:

  • Application fee: ₹10 (can be paid through cash, demand draft, banker's cheque, or IPO)
  • Fee for information: ₹2 per page created or copied
  • Fee for information in printed form: Actual cost of publication
  • No fee for persons below poverty line (BPL)
  • No fee for electronic information

The rules define the decision-making process by the Commission:

  1. Notice of hearing to the parties at least seven clear days before the date of hearing
  2. Appellant or respondent may be present in person or through their authorized representative
  3. Commission may examine documents or accept oral evidence
  4. Commission may impose penalties as per Section 20 of the Act
  5. Decision to be authenticated by the Information Commissioner who heard the appeal

NOTIFICATION

New Delhi, the 31st July, 2012

1. Short title and commencement:

(1) These rules may be called the Right to Information Rules, 2012.
