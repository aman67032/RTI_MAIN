Understanding RTI Rules and Regulations

The Right to Information Act, 2005 empowers citizens to request information from public authorities. To implement this Act effectively, various rules and regulations have been framed by Central and State Governments. These rules specify the procedures, forms, and fees for seeking information, as well as the obligations of public authorities in responding to RTI requests.

While the RTI Act provides the overarching framework, the rules address the procedural aspects of implementing the law. Different states may have variations in their rules, reflecting local contexts and administrative structures, but all must align with the fundamental principles of the Act.

Central RTI Rules

Rules framed by the Central Government that apply to all Union Ministries, Departments, and Public Authorities

State RTI Rules

Rules formulated by individual State Governments that apply to public authorities within their respective states

Public Authority Rules

Internal guidelines and procedures adopted by specific public authorities to handle RTI applications

Central RTI Rules, 2012

Rule 1 - Short Title and Commencement

(1) These rules may be called the Right to Information Rules, 2012.

(2) They shall come into force on the date of their publication in the Official Gazette.

Rule 2 - Definitions

In these rules, unless the context otherwise requires:

  1. "Act" means the Right to Information Act, 2005 (22 of 2005);
  2. "Commission" means the Central Information Commission;
  3. "First Appellate Authority" means an officer designated as such under sub-section (1) of section 19 of the Act;
  4. "Registrar" means the Registrar of the Commission;
  5. "Section" means a section of the Act;
  6. Words and expressions used herein but not defined shall have the same meanings as assigned to them in the Act.

Rule 3 - Application Fee

An application under sub-section (1) of Section 6 of the Act shall be accompanied by a fee of rupees ten and shall be made in Form A.

The fee may be paid in any of the following manners:

  1. By cash to the public authority against proper receipt; or
  2. By demand draft/banker's cheque/Indian Postal Order payable to the Accounts Officer of the public authority; or
  3. By electronic means to the Accounts Officer of the public authority, if facility for accepting fees through electronic means is available with the public authority.

Educational Note

The application fee is nominal to ensure affordability. BPL (Below Poverty Line) card holders are exempt from paying any fees to access information under the RTI Act. Additionally, many public authorities now accept online payment of fees through payment gateways to simplify the process.

Rule 4 - Format of RTI Application

The application should be addressed to the Public Information Officer (PIO) of the concerned public authority. It should contain:

  1. Name of the applicant
  2. Address for communication
  3. Particulars of information sought
  4. Whether the applicant is below poverty line (if claiming fee exemption)

The application need not state any reason for requesting the information or any other personal details except those necessary for contacting the applicant.

RTI Rules for Educational Institutions

Application to Educational Institutions

All government-run educational institutions and private institutions that receive substantial government funding fall under the purview of the RTI Act as public authorities. These include:

  1. Government schools and colleges
  2. Universities and deemed universities
  3. Educational boards (CBSE, State Boards)
  4. Private institutions receiving substantial government aid

Information Disclosure Requirements

Educational institutions must proactively disclose:

  1. Basic organizational information including functions and duties
  2. Powers and duties of faculty and administrative staff
  3. Decision-making procedures and accountability channels
  4. Rules, regulations, instructions, and manuals
  5. Budget allocation and expenditure details
  6. Scholarship and financial aid information
  7. Admission procedures and criteria
  8. Fee structure and any additional charges

Educational Institution Responsibilities

Schools and colleges must designate a Public Information Officer (PIO) responsible for providing information to citizens. They should maintain records in a manner that facilitates the right to information, and should be computerized where possible for wider accessibility. Educational institutions must update their proactive disclosures periodically.

Student Records and Privacy

Educational institutions must balance transparency with privacy concerns:

  1. Information that relates to personal information, the disclosure of which would cause unwarranted invasion of privacy, is exempt unless larger public interest justifies disclosure
  2. Evaluation records and academic scores can be disclosed to the concerned student
  3. Aggregate data about pass percentages, placement statistics, etc., should be proactively disclosed
  4. Information about administrative decisions affecting students collectively should be accessible

Fee Structure and Payment Rules

Application Fee

The standard application fee under Central Rules is Rs. 10 per application. However, state rules may prescribe different amounts, generally ranging from Rs. 10 to Rs. 50. Below Poverty Line (BPL) cardholders are exempt from paying any fee.

Additional Costs

For providing information under sub-section (1) of Section 7, the fee shall be charged at the following rates:

  1. Rs. 2 for each page created or copied in A4 or A3 size paper
  2. Actual cost for samples, models, or photocopies in larger paper sizes
  3. Actual cost or price for information in printed publications
  4. Rs. 50 per diskette or floppy for information provided in electronic format
  5. Actual cost for information provided in CD/DVD/other electronic format
  6. Actual postal charges for mailing the information

Important Note for Applicants

The PIO will inform the applicant about the additional fees (if any) required to process the RTI request. The period between the dispatch of this intimation and payment of fees shall be excluded from the 30-day time limit for providing information. No fee shall be charged from BPL applicants or for information delayed beyond the prescribed time limit.

Appeal Procedures and Rules

First Appeal

If an applicant does not receive information within 30 days or is dissatisfied with the information provided, they can file a first appeal to the First Appellate Authority (an officer senior to the PIO).

  1. The first appeal must be filed within 30 days from the expiry of the prescribed time limit or receipt of decision from the PIO
  2. The First Appellate Authority must dispose of the appeal within 30 days, which may be extended to 45 days with reasons recorded in writing
  3. No fee is charged for filing the first appeal

Second Appeal

If the applicant is still dissatisfied with the decision of the First Appellate Authority, they can file a second appeal to the Information Commission (Central or State):

  1. The second appeal must be filed within 90 days from the receipt of the decision of the First Appellate Authority
  2. The appeal should contain verified copies of the orders against which the appeal is made
  3. The Information Commission can admit appeals filed after the 90-day period if satisfied that the appellant had sufficient cause for the delay

Complaint Procedure

Apart from appeals, any person can file a complaint directly to the Information Commission if they have been unable to submit an RTI application because a PIO has not been appointed, or if the PIO has refused to accept the application, or if the required assistance for filing an application has been denied.
