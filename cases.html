Case Study 01
Landmark

RTI for Exam Paper Re-Evaluation (CBSE v. Aditya Bandopadhyay, 2011)

Filed by: Aditya Bandopadhyay
Against: Central Board of Secondary Education
Filed in: 2011
Supreme Court

Issue:

  • CBSE students faced errors in their board exam marks but were denied access to their answer sheets.
  • The CBSE claimed that answer sheets were "confidential" and not subject to RTI requests.

RTI Intervention:

  • Aditya Bandopadhyay, a student, filed an RTI requesting his evaluated answer sheet.
  • CBSE rejected the request, which led to a legal battle in the Supreme Court.
  • The Supreme Court ruled in favor of the students, stating that exam answer sheets fall under the scope of RTI.

Impact:

  • Students can now request copies of their evaluated answer sheets under RTI.
  • Exposed marking errors and unfair evaluation practices.
  • Led to significant changes in CBSE's policy, increasing transparency in re-evaluation.

Recent

RTI for NEET Paper Leak Investigation (2024)

Filed by: Dr. Vivek Pandey
Against: National Testing Agency (NTA)
Filed in: 2024
Impacted: 24+ lakh candidates

Issue:

  • In May 2024, allegations arose regarding the leak of the NEET-UG exam paper, impacting over 24 lakh candidates.
  • Students demanded proof of a fair investigation, but the National Testing Agency (NTA) refused to provide information, citing "security reasons."

RTI Intervention:

  • Dr. Vivek Pandey, a health activist, filed an RTI requesting a status report on the investigation from the Bihar Director General of Police.
  • The Central Bureau of Investigation (CBI) denied disclosing investigation details, citing exemptions under the RTI Act, which raised debates on transparency.

Impact:

  • The Supreme Court ruled against conducting a retest, urging authorities to improve cybersecurity and identity verification measures.
  • The incident led to the enforcement of the Public Examinations (Prevention of Unfair Means) Act, 2024, which aims to enhance the integrity of public exams.

Case Study 03
Significant

RTI for Admission & Reservation Transparency (Delhi University, 2018)

Filed by: Students and Activists
Against: Delhi University
Filed in: 2018
OBC Quota: 27%

Issue:

  • OBC applicants faced difficulties during Delhi University admissions, with unclear quota implementations and admission delays.
  • Authorities were reluctant to provide clear information about cut-offs and seat allocations.

RTI Intervention:

  • Students and activists filed RTI applications seeking details on OBC category admissions, cut-offs, and seat matrices.
  • The responses revealed inconsistencies in seat allotment and lack of adherence to the mandated 27% OBC reservation policy.

Impact:

  • The exposure led to increased pressure on Delhi University to streamline its admission process for OBC students.
  • It highlighted systemic gaps and the need for stronger monitoring and transparency in implementing reservation policies.

Case Study 04
Landmark

RTI for RTE Implementation in Private Schools (2012)

Filed by: Social Rights Activists
Against: Multiple State Education Departments
Filed in: 2012
Impact: Nationwide

Issue:

  • Private schools were found to be non-compliant with the 25% reservation for economically weaker sections required by the Right to Education Act.
  • Schools were either not implementing the quota or creating artificial barriers for disadvantaged students.
  • State education departments were not enforcing compliance or monitoring implementation.

RTI Intervention:

  • RTI applications filed across multiple states seeking data on RTE quota implementation in private schools.
  • Information sought included number of seats reserved, admission procedures, and action taken against non-compliant schools.
  • The case eventually reached the Supreme Court when several private schools challenged the RTE Act itself.

Impact:

  • Supreme Court upheld the constitutional validity of the RTE Act, requiring private schools to comply with the 25% reservation.
  • Led to the establishment of regulatory committees in states to monitor implementation.
  • Improved admission chances for over 2 million disadvantaged children annually across India.
  • Created a framework for ongoing monitoring through RTI-based social audits.

Significant

RTI for Mid-Day Meal Scheme Transparency (2013-2014)

Filed by: NGO Collective
Against: Human Resource Development Ministry
Filed in: 2013
Impacted: 120 million children

Issue:

  • Reports of widespread corruption in the implementation of the Mid-Day Meal Scheme across multiple states.
  • Concerns over food quality, safety, and nutritional adequacy after multiple food poisoning incidents.
  • Discrepancies between allocated funds, reported beneficiaries, and actual meal distribution.

RTI Intervention:

  • A series of coordinated RTI applications filed across 10 states seeking detailed expenditure reports, supplier contracts, and quality inspection records.
  • Information requested on testing protocols, food safety certifications, and incident reporting mechanisms.
  • Findings compiled into a comprehensive public report highlighting systemic issues in the scheme's implementation.

Impact:

  • Led to the creation of a centralized online monitoring system for real-time tracking of meal distribution.
  • Resulted in revised nutritional guidelines and mandatory food safety certifications for suppliers.
  • Introduced community-based monitoring committees with parent representatives.
  • Estimated to have improved meal quality for over 120 million school children across India.

Case Study 06
Active

RTI for Teacher Recruitment & Qualification Verification (2022-Present)

Filed by: Education Reform Collective
Against: State Public Service Commissions
Ongoing since: 2022
Concerns: 35,000+ appointments

Issue:

  • Allegations of widespread irregularities in teacher recruitment processes across multiple states.
  • Reports of appointments made without proper verification of qualifications and teaching credentials.
  • Concerns about political influence in the selection process and violations of merit-based recruitment policies.

RTI Intervention:

  • Multiple RTI applications filed seeking detailed information on selection criteria, interview scores, and verification procedures.
  • Requests for disclosure of appointment committee compositions and declaration of conflicts of interest.
  • Information sought on actions taken against appointments found to be based on fraudulent qualifications.

Impact:

  • Ongoing investigations have already identified over 2,800 potentially fraudulent appointments.
  • Several state governments have initiated re-verification of teacher qualifications.
  • Development of an online transparency portal showing recruitment scores and qualification details.
  • Policy changes mandating regular audits of teacher recruitment processes.

Case Study 07
Significant

RTI for Medical College Fee Structure Transparency (2019)

Filed by: Parent Association
Against: Fee Regulatory Committee & Private Medical Colleges
Filed in: 2019
Impact: Fee reduction of 15-30%

Issue:

  • Private medical colleges in multiple states were charging exorbitant fees despite government regulations.
  • Lack of transparency in how fee structures were determined and approved by regulatory committees.
  • Unauthorized collection of capitation fees and other hidden charges despite court rulings against the practice.

RTI Intervention:

  • RTI applications filed seeking detailed breakdowns of fee structures, committee meeting minutes, and financial audits.
  • Information requested on cost calculation methodologies and justification for annual fee increases.
  • Data sought on actual infrastructure and faculty expenses versus claims made by institutions.

Impact:

  • High Court ordered an independent audit of 35 private medical colleges, revealing significant financial discrepancies.
  • Fee Regulatory Committee implemented a standardized transparent formula for calculating permissible fees.
  • Resulted in fee reductions of 15-30% across multiple institutions benefiting thousands of students.
  • Established mandatory public disclosure of detailed fee breakdowns and financial statements.
