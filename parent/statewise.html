Why State-Specific Education Information Matters

Education in India operates under a concurrent framework, with both central and state governments having authority. State education laws, regulations, and policies vary significantly, making it essential for parents to understand the specific rules governing their child's education in their state.

Key Benefits of Our Statewise Database:

  • Access state-specific education acts and regulations
  • Compare fee regulation mechanisms across states
  • Find state education department RTI filing procedures
  • Discover school safety standards for your state
  • Understand RTE implementation in your region

Featured State Education Profiles

Delhi

  • Fee Regulation: Delhi School Education Act, 1973
  • Key Authority: Directorate of Education (DoE)
  • RTI Filing: Public Information Officer, DoE
  • Notable Rule: Schools on public land need DOE approval for fee hikes
View Full Delhi Profile

Maharashtra

  • Fee Regulation: Maharashtra Educational Institutions (Regulation of Fee) Act, 2011
  • Key Authority: Divisional Fee Regulatory Committee
  • RTI Filing: Education Department, Maharashtra Government
  • Notable Rule: 15% maximum fee increase every two years
View Full Maharashtra Profile

Tamil Nadu

  • Fee Regulation: Tamil Nadu Schools (Regulation of Collection of Fee) Act, 2009
  • Key Authority: Fee Determination Committee
  • RTI Filing: Chief Educational Officer of the district
  • Notable Rule: Schools classified into tiers based on facilities
View Full Tamil Nadu Profile
