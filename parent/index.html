Understanding Your Rights as a Parent

The Right to Information Act (RTI), 2005, empowers you as a parent to ensure quality education for your child. Under Article 21-A of the Indian Constitution and the Right to Education Act, 2009, every child between 6-14 years has a fundamental right to free and compulsory education.

Legal Framework

RTI Act Section 2(h) covers all schools - government, private, and aided institutions under its purview.

Financial Transparency

Right to access information about fees, funds utilization, and development charges.

Safety & Standards

Right to verify safety measures, infrastructure standards, and teacher qualifications.

Equal Opportunity

Right to fair admission process and protection against discrimination.

What You Can Know:

  • School's recognition status and affiliation details
  • Teacher qualifications and student-teacher ratio
  • Fee structure and financial statements
  • Infrastructure and safety compliance
  • Admission criteria and selection process
  • Implementation of government schemes

Your RTI application can be a powerful tool for:

  • Ensuring accountability in school administration
  • Protecting your child's educational rights
  • Contributing to better educational standards
  • Creating transparency in the education system
