Understanding Government Educational Schemes

The Indian government, at both central and state levels, operates numerous educational schemes and programs to promote quality education, increase access, and provide financial assistance to students. Many parents remain unaware of these benefits, or face challenges in accessing them due to information gaps and implementation issues.

Why Track Government Schemes:

  • Access scholarships and financial assistance for your child
  • Ensure your child's school implements mandatory programs
  • Monitor proper utilization of scheme funds in your school
  • Understand eligibility criteria and application processes
  • Hold authorities accountable for scheme implementation

Major Central Government Schemes

Samagra Shiksha Abhiyan

  • Type: Integrated Education Program
  • Coverage: Pre-school to Class 12
  • Benefits: School infrastructure, quality enhancement, teacher training
  • Authority: Ministry of Education

PM POSHAN (Mid-Day Meal)

  • Type: Nutrition Program
  • Coverage: Pre-primary to Class 8
  • Benefits: Hot cooked meal with prescribed nutritional standards

PM-SHRI Schools

  • Type: Model School Development

Scholarship Programs

National Means-cum-Merit Scholarship

For Class 8 students with family income below ₹3.5 lakh annually; provides ₹12,000 per year for Class 9-12

Pre-Matric Scholarships

For SC/ST/OBC/Minority students in Classes 9-10; covers course fees, maintenance allowance

Post-Matric Scholarships

For SC/ST/OBC/Minority students in Classes 11-12 and higher education; covers full course fees

Central Sector Scheme

Merit-based scholarships for college students; offers ₹10,000 per annum to top 0.5% of students

INSPIRE Scholarships

For science students; ₹80,000 per year for pursuing BSc and MSc in natural and basic sciences

Pragati & Saksham

For technical education; Pragati for girls and Saksham for differently-abled students

PM POSHAN (Mid-Day Meal) Entitlements

Nutritional Standards

Primary Level (Class I-V)

  • Calories: Minimum 450 kcal
  • Protein: Minimum 12g
  • Rice/Wheat: 100g per child
  • Pulses: 20g per child
  • Vegetables: 50g per child
  • Oil & Fat: 5g per child

Upper Primary Level (Class VI-VIII)

  • Calories: Minimum 700 kcal
  • Protein: Minimum 20g
  • Rice/Wheat: 150g per child
  • Pulses: 30g per child
  • Vegetables: 75g per child
  • Oil & Fat: 7.5g per child

Additional Provisions

Implementation Schedule

  • Hot cooked meal every working day
  • Minimum 200 working days per year
  • No disruption during school vacations in drought-affected areas
  • Food security allowance if meal not provided

Quality Safeguards

  • Regular health check-ups
  • Measuring height & weight of children
  • Micronutrient supplementation
  • De-worming tablets biannually
  • Mandatory testing of meals

Government Scheme Implementation Success Stories

Our parent committee discovered through RTI that our school received ₹3.2 lakhs for library books under Samagra Shiksha, but no new books had been added in two years. After presenting this evidence to the District Education Officer, the school was directed to procure the books within a month, benefiting over 800 students.

Parent-Teacher Association

Government High School, Lucknow

Using RTI, we found that only 42 out of 108 eligible students in our village had received their scholarships due to application processing delays. With this information, we worked with school authorities to expedite the remaining applications, ensuring all eligible students received their entitlements.

Village Education Committee

Raipur District, Chhattisgarh
