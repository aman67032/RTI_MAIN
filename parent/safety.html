School Safety Rights & Guidelines

Every child has the right to a safe learning environment. Multiple guidelines, including those from education boards, state governments, and the Supreme Court of India, mandate comprehensive safety measures in schools.

Your Child's Rights to School Safety:

  • Right to physical safety and infrastructure security
  • Right to protection from harassment and bullying
  • Right to clean drinking water and hygienic sanitation facilities
  • Right to safe transportation if school transport is used
  • Right to emergency medical facilities and first aid

Key Safety Concerns in Schools

Infrastructure Safety

Issues with building safety, fire safety equipment, emergency exits, and hazardous areas

Child Protection

Prevention of abuse, harassment, and bullying through proper screening and complaint mechanisms

Transport Safety

Standards for school buses and vans including qualified drivers, attendants, and GPS tracking

Medical Facilities

Availability of school nurses, first-aid kits, and emergency response protocols

Hygiene Standards

Access to clean drinking water, functional toilets, and proper waste management

Access Controls

Measures to prevent unauthorized entry and protocols for visitor management

Essential Safety Regulations for Schools

Fire Safety Requirements

  • Valid No-Objection Certificate (NOC) from Fire Department
  • Working fire extinguishers at key locations
  • Fire alarm systems and smoke detectors
  • Regular fire drills and evacuation plans

Staff Verification

  • Police verification of all staff members
  • Background checks for transport staff
  • Psychometric evaluation of teachers (CBSE requirement)
  • Training in child protection policies

CCTV Surveillance

  • Coverage in all public areas and corridors
  • Functional cameras in school transport
  • Minimum 60-day footage retention
  • Regular monitoring and maintenance

CBSE/Education Board Safety Guidelines

Area Key Requirements Compliance Indicators
Building Safety Structural stability certificate, occupancy certificate, regular building audits Valid certificates from appropriate authorities, inspection records, maintenance logs
Transport Safety GPS tracking, speed governors, qualified drivers, female attendants RTO-certified school buses, driver verification, functioning GPS system
Sexual Harassment Prevention POCSO awareness, Gender Sensitization Committee, complaint box Committee formation orders, awareness training records, investigation protocols
Medical Facilities School nurse/doctor, sickroom, first-aid kits, emergency protocols Qualified health staff, equipped medical room, student health records
Disaster Management Evacuation plans, regular drills, early warning systems Displayed evacuation maps, drill records, trained safety marshals
Cyber Safety Internet filters, monitoring systems, cyber awareness programs Filtering software, usage policies, student training records

Note: These guidelines may be updated periodically. Parents should check the official CBSE website or respective education board for the latest requirements.

How to Use RTI to Ensure School Safety

1

Identify Safety Concerns

Observe and document specific safety issues in your child's school that need addressing

2

File RTI Application

Submit an RTI to education departments, boards, or relevant public authorities overseeing school safety

3

Request Safety Certifications

Ask for copies of safety certificates, inspection reports, and compliance documentation

4

Form Parent Safety Committee

Collaborate with other concerned parents to review findings and develop an action plan

5

Engage with School Management

Present findings to school authorities with specific recommendations for improvement

Key Court Orders on School Safety

Supreme Court Guidelines on School Bus Safety (2017)

These guidelines mandate:

  • Yellow painted buses with clear "SCHOOL BUS" markings
  • First-aid box, fire extinguisher, and emergency exits
  • Speed governors limited to 40 km/h
  • Qualified drivers with 5+ years of experience
  • Mandatory female attendant for buses with girl students
Writ Petition (Civil) No. 13029 of 1985

Delhi High Court Order on School Safety (2016)

This order requires schools to:

  • Install CCTV cameras covering all areas except washrooms
  • Ensure GPS tracking in all school transport
  • Conduct police verification of all staff
  • Establish clearly defined protocols for entry/exit of visitors
W.P.(C) 8886/2010

Supreme Court Directives on POCSO Implementation (2018)

These directives mandate:

  • Rigorous screening process for hiring school staff
  • Sexual harassment committees with student representatives
  • Mandatory POCSO awareness sessions for all staff and students
  • Age-appropriate safety education for children
Writ Petition (Civil) No. 565 of 2012
