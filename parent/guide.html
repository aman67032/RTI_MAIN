Understanding the Right to Information Act

The Right to Information Act, 2005 empowers Indian citizens to request information from public authorities. As a parent, you can use this powerful tool to ensure transparency and accountability in your child's educational institution.

What is RTI?

  • RTI stands for Right to Information, a fundamental right of Indian citizens
  • It allows you to request and receive information from government bodies and public authorities
  • Schools, both government and private (receiving substantial government aid), fall under RTI Act
  • Information must be provided within 30 days of application

Sample RTI Questions for Education

Fee Structure

"Please provide details of the fee structure for all classes for the current academic year, along with the basis for determining these fees."

Teacher Qualifications

"Please provide information regarding the qualification, experience, and appointment details of all teachers in the school."

Curriculum Details

"Please provide information about the curriculum followed, including books prescribed and assessment methods."

Safety Measures

"Please provide details of all safety measures implemented in the school, including fire safety, transportation safety, and staff verification."

Points to Remember

  • Be specific in your request
  • Avoid asking questions (ask for information)
  • Keep a copy of all correspondence
  • File one RTI per issue

Your RTI Rights

  • Right to receive information within 30 days
  • Right to file first appeal if unsatisfied
  • Right to assistance in filing RTI
  • Right to inspect documents

Information Exemptions

  • Information affecting national security
  • Personal information invading privacy
  • Information protected by copyright
  • Cabinet papers
