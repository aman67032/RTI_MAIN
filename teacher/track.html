RTI Application Timeline

Understanding the typical timeline of an RTI application can help you track its progress effectively.

1

Application Submission

Application is submitted to the Public Information Officer (PIO) with the requisite fee.

Day 0
2

Acknowledgment

PIO provides an acknowledgment receipt with a unique registration number.

Within 7 days
3

Processing

PIO processes the request and collects the required information from relevant departments.

Day 7-25
4

Response

PIO provides the requested information or reasons for denial if applicable.

Within 30 days
5

First Appeal

If response is unsatisfactory or not received, applicant can file a first appeal.

Within 30 days of response deadline
6

Second Appeal

If first appeal is unsuccessful, applicant can file a second appeal with the Information Commission.

Within 90 days of first appeal decision

Frequently Asked Questions

What information do I need to track my RTI application?

To track your RTI application, you'll need your registration number which is provided in your acknowledgment receipt. In some cases, you may also need the password or PIN that was created during the filing process.

How long does it take to get a response to my RTI application?

According to the RTI Act, the Public Information Officer (PIO) is required to provide information within 30 days of receiving the application. In case the information concerns the life or liberty of a person, it should be provided within 48 hours.

What should I do if I don't receive any response to my RTI application?

If you don't receive a response within the stipulated time period (30 days), you can file a first appeal with the First Appellate Authority (FAA) of the concerned department. The FAA is required to respond to your appeal within 30 days.

Can I track multiple RTI applications with the same account?

Yes, if you've filed multiple RTI applications through the RTI Online Portal using the same account, you can track all of them by logging into your account. Each application will have its own unique registration number for individual tracking.

What happens if my RTI application is transferred to another department?

If your application is transferred to another department under Section 6(3) of the RTI Act, the 30-day timeline will start from the date the application is received by the appropriate authority. You should receive a notification about the transfer, and you can continue tracking using the same registration number.
