About RTI Forms

Filing an effective RTI application requires clarity, precision, and proper formatting. On this page, you'll find ready-to-use templates specifically designed for teachers and education professionals to address common information needs in the education sector.

Important Note

Before filing an RTI, consider these points:

  • Be specific about the information you need
  • Identify the correct public authority
  • Include the required application fee
  • Keep your questions clear and focused
  • Ask for specific documents rather than general information

Frequently Asked Questions

How much is the RTI application fee?

The standard RTI application fee is â‚¹10. For BPL (Below Poverty Line) applicants, there is no fee. Additional charges may apply if you request information in a specific format or if photocopies exceed a certain number of pages.

How many questions can I ask in one RTI application?

While there is no legal limit to the number of questions, it is advisable to keep your application focused on a single subject with 4-5 specific questions. Too many unrelated questions might lead to incomplete responses or rejection.

What if I don't receive a response within 30 days?

If you don't receive a response within 30 days, you can file a first appeal with the First Appellate Authority of the same department. The details of the First Appellate Authority are usually available on the department's website.

Can I file RTI online?

Yes, for central government departments, you can file RTI applications online through the RTI Online Portal (https://rtionline.gov.in/). Many state governments also have their own online RTI portals. The fee can be paid online through various payment methods.
